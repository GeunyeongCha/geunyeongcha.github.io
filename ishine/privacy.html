Privacy Policy

Effective date : July 3, 2025

1 · Overview

[App Name] (“App,” “we,” “our”) is an academic research tool developed by [Institution]. This policy explains what information we collect from you, why we collect it, and how we protect it.

2 · Information We Collect

  1. Fitbit data (with consent). Heart-rate, step count, sleep metrics, activity sessions, and battery level.
  2. Account details. Email address or study ID, hashed password, consent timestamp.
  3. Device & usage data. IP address, browser or mobile OS type, crash logs, and basic analytics (no advertising identifiers).

3 · How We Use Your Information

  • Research. To analyze physical-activity patterns and heart-health outcomes for peer-reviewed publication.
  • App delivery. To authenticate you, display summaries of your Fitbit data, and troubleshoot technical issues.
  • Regulatory compliance. To satisfy Institutional Review Board (IRB) monitoring and legal obligations.

4 · Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data only with your informed consent (per the signed Consent Form) or where we have a legitimate interest in maintaining the App’s security and integrity.

5 · Information Sharing & Disclosure

  1. No sale or advertising use. We never sell or rent your personal data, and we do not use it for targeted ads.
  2. Service providers. AWS (cloud hosting) and Fitbit (data source) process data under strict contractual controls.
  3. Research publications. Only anonymized, aggregated results are published—never data that can identify you.
  4. Legal or ethical requirements. We may disclose data if mandated by law or to protect participants’ vital interests.

6 · Data Retention

Identifiable data are stored on encrypted servers in the United States for up to 5 years after your last data sync, then permanently deleted or de-identified. You may request earlier deletion at any time (see § 9).

7 · Security Measures

All data in transit use HTTPS/TLS 1.2+; at rest, records are encrypted with AES-256. Access is restricted to authorized research personnel who have completed HIPAA and data-security training.

8 · Children’s Privacy

The study is intended for adults 18 years and older. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has provided information, contact us for prompt deletion.

9 · Your Choices & Rights

  • Access / download. Request a copy of your Fitbit-sourced data and study responses.
  • Correction. Ask us to correct inaccurate account details.
  • Deletion / withdrawal. Disconnect Fitbit or email us to have all identifiable data erased and participation ended—no questions asked.

10 · International Data Transfers

If you access the App from outside the United States, be aware your data will be processed and stored on U.S. servers, subject to U.S. privacy laws.

11 · Policy Updates

We may revise this policy to reflect changes in research scope or legal requirements. Any material change will be posted here and, when feasible, sent via email. The “Effective date” above will always show the latest version.
